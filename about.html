Rating System

Each annotated span includes three key attributes, each rated on a continuous 0.0-1.0 scale for precise measurement:

  • Evidence (0.0-1.0): How well-supported the claim or statement is by available data. Higher values indicate stronger empirical support.
  • Likelihood (0.0-1.0): The probability that the annotated claim or event will occur. 0.0 means highly unlikely, 1.0 means certain to occur.
  • Consequence (0.0-1.0): The potential impact or importance of the annotated content. Higher values indicate more significant consequences.

Visual Indicators

The tool uses color-coded progress bars to make ratings immediately understandable:

  • Evidence Bars: Green indicates high confidence (strong evidence), Red indicates low confidence
  • Likelihood Bars: Red indicates high probability (higher risk), Green indicates low probability
  • Consequence Bars: Red indicates high impact (severe consequences), Green indicates low impact

Purpose and Applications

This tool is designed for researchers, analysts, and anyone interested in understanding the reliability and impact of information. Key applications include:

  • Research Analysis: Evaluate the strength of evidence behind different claims in academic or policy documents
  • Risk Assessment: Understand both the likelihood and potential consequences of various scenarios
  • Information Quality: Quickly identify which parts of a text are well-supported versus speculative
  • Decision Support: Use quantified ratings to inform decision-making processes
